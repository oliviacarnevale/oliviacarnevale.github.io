C.J. Stroud, Should-Have-Been Heisman Winner?

Author

Olivia Carnevale

Published

April 16, 2023

Former Ohio State quarterback C.J. Stroud had a successful career with the Buckeyes, winning a Rose Bowl in 2021, leading his team to the College Football Playoff in 2022, and remains a top prospect in the 2023 NFL Draft. One accomplishment he didn’t aqiure: a Heisman Trophy.

In his two years as a starting quarterback at Ohio State, Stroud was nominated twice as a Heisman Trophy Finalist. In 2021, he finished fourth in voting behind Alabama quarterback Bryce Young, Michigan defensive lineman Aiden Hutchinson and Pitt quarterback Kenny Pickett. Again, in 2022, Stroud failed to get enough votes to win placing third behind USC quarterback Caleb Williams and TCU quarterback Max Duggan, but just ahead of Georgia quarterback Stetson Bennett. 

But should Stroud have finished so low in comparison to the other five quarterbacks in the respective Heisman races? 

Let’s start by looking at his passing statistics in both the 2021 and 2022 seasons.

Stroud’s completion percentage shows how accurate he was in both college football seasons. In 2022, he was in the middle of the pack in Heisman finalists, but in 2021, he was far out in front of any other Heisman finalist from either year. 

The closest finalist to him in the two-year span of completion percentage? The one quarterback who finished behind him in 2022, Stetson Bennett. The 2021 winner? Bryce Young, a whole five percent lower than him in completion percentage.

Completion percentage isn’t the only thing that qualifies a quarterback as an arguable Heisman winner, however. Let’s dive further into Stroud’s passing statistics in relation to touchdowns and interceptions. 

In both 2021 and 2022 Stroud finished just below the respective year’s Heisman winner in passing touchdown to interception ratio. However, in 2021, Bryce Young’s end-of-season seven interceptions is almost identical to Stroud’s six. In fact, Young’s three more touchdowns came from playing three more games, and the Heisman race is decided before postseason play.

So 2021 Stroud seemed as though he should have been at the top of the argument for the most accurate and highest scoring regular-season quarterback in college football, but what about yards?

Again, in 2021 Stroud finished far in front of the Heisman pack in passing yards per game, but struggled in rushing yards per game. Who else struggled rushing? Bryce Young, the 2021 Heisman winner.

Stroud seemed to have a slight edge in completion percentage, regular season touchdown to interception ratio, and, now, passing yards per game over Young, but should rushing yards per game really have pulled him down that far in 2021 Heisman voting?

Let’s not count 2022 Stroud out, however. Did he again deserve to again finish so low in Heisman voting?

In both 2021 and 2022 Stroud led his conference and both years’ Heisman finalists in passing touchdowns per game.

Code 
set.seed(1234)

ptd <- both %>%
  mutate(
    passtdpergame = PassingTD/G
  ) 

heisman <- c("Bryce Young 2021", "C.J. Stroud 2021", "C.J. Stroud 2022", "Caleb Williams 2022", "Max Duggan 2022", "Stetson Bennett 2022", "Kenny Pickett 2021")

td <- ptd %>% filter(PlayerSeason %in% heisman) %>% 
  select(PlayerSeason, passtdpergame, Conf)

So, in 2021 Stroud was clearly the most accurate quarterback in the Heisman race and accounted for the most passing touchdowns and passing yards per game, and in 2022, kept his title as the Heisman finalist with the most touchdowns per game.

More simply put, in both 2021 and 2022 C.J. Stroud appeared to be one of, if not the best, passer in college football. Yet he finished fourth and third in Heisman voting over his two years starting at Ohio State.

These statistics prompt the question, did Stroud put on a Heisman-deserving performance at either year at Ohio State? Specific to his 2021 statistics, was Stroud robbed of the Heisman in 2021 after putting on such great passing performances, better performances than even Bryce Young?

That’s up to you.
